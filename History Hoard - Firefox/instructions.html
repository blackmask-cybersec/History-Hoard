HistoryHoard Instructions Populate Export Contact
  1. The 'populate' button fetches every URL and its corresponding visits currently stored by the browser and adds it to the extension's own database on your computer. Click this button the first time you run the extension. Note - On slower devices, this process could take several minutes and will likely cause your browser to stutter. So plan accordingly.
  2. Every time the browser launches, the extension grabs the latest 2,500 URLs and their visits stored by the browser and updates the extension database accordingly.
  3. If you think you've made over 2,500 visits since the last time you launched Firefox, use the 'populate' button again to get all URLs and visits stored by the browser.
  4. Usually, you would only need to use the 'populate' button once i.e. the time you first download the extension. The extension should manage the rest.
  5. As of April 2022, Firefox allows an extension to store up to 2GB of data before evicting it. This would mean that your data could be stored on your computer for decades hassle-free.
  6. Alas, Firefox could change their browser storage limits and eviction criteria. Therefore, I recommend using the export functionality every 6 months or so to ensure your data remains safe with you.
